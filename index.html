Design Research

This showcase platform was developed to fill a gap in the College of Arts & Media’s digital presence. While CAM produces outstanding student and alumni work, there was no centralized, professional space for this work to be featured online. Our research revealed that students benefit both professionally and personally from seeing their work displayed. Interviews and analysis showed the need for a platform that is community driven, educational, and easy to update.

Our Findings Also Revealed:
• Digital showcases help connect students and alumni with recruiters and professionals.
• Visibility increases student motivation and belonging.
• Community engagement fosters collaboration and growth.

Our design serves students, alumni, faculty, and professionals, each with unique goals, from showcasing work to discovering talent.

Project Overview

A centralized platform for showcasing CAM student and alumni work to improve visibility and connection.

Target Audience

Defined primary users: students, alumni, faculty, industry professionals, and prospective students

User Goals

Focused on exposure, networking, feedback, and showcasing work to foster community.

Design Research

Used interviews, research, and competitive analysis to understand user needs and platform gaps.

User Interviews

Insightful conversations with real users revealed features and functions they valued most.

Persona Development

Key user types like students, alumni, and faculty shaped the platform’s features and flow.

Competitive Analysis

Reviewed Behance, Adobe Portfolio, and WordPress to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities.

Moodboard

A collection of visuals, colors, and styles that inspired the platform’s creative direction.

Wireframe Sketches

Early stage, hand drawn layouts for mobile, tablet, and desktop versions of the site.
